*Notebook / private markets*

Most Canadian private market participants operate with variations of the same playbook.

The differences are smaller than they appear.

Deal flow is intermediated through the same channels. Capital is structured under similar constraints. “Quality” is defined using overlapping criteria.

This creates a form of convergence.

Not in strategy, but in behavior.

The same types of businesses are pursued:

- recurring revenue
- asset-light
- defensible niches

The same risks are accepted:

- moderate concentration
- limited scalability
- dependence on key individuals

And the same assumptions are underwritten.

Individually, each decision is rational.

Collectively, they compress differentiation.

Over time, this leads to:

- crowded positioning in “safe” assets
- pricing that reflects consensus more than conviction
- reduced margin for error when conditions shift

The risk is not that deals are poorly analyzed.

It’s that they are analyzed in the same way.

When everyone agrees a deal is attractive, pricing is rarely the only thing that has converged.

The real risk is not bad deals.

It’s identical thinking.
