There is a fashionable obsession with “velocity” in private markets. Fine—execution matters. But velocity without kill criteria is just expensive commitment.

The best processes I have seen share an ugly trait: they preserve the right to be wrong *for a bounded window*. Hypotheses are explicit. Spend is staged. Reversibility is negotiated up front, before ego attaches to the plan.

Contrast that with the polite alternative: endless decks, ambiguous milestones, and a consensus that pretends uncertainty has been voted away. That environment does not reduce risk; it socializes it until nobody feels accountable when reality intrudes.

If your diligence cannot name what would make you stop, you are not derisking. You are delaying embarrassment.
