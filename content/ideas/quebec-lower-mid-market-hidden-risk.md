*Notebook / private markets*

A large portion of Quebec’s lower mid-market is perceived as conservative.

Founder-owned businesses. Recurring revenue. “Boring” sectors.

On the surface, this reads as stability.

In reality, much of that stability is conditional.

What looks like recurring revenue is often recurring effort.

Customer relationships are concentrated, but not always contractual. Retention depends less on product and more on long-standing trust with the founder.

What looks like operational discipline is often dependency.

Lean teams, minimal overhead, and centralized decision-making create efficiency—but also fragility.

The business works because the founder is still there.

Not because the system works without them.

This becomes visible only at transition.

When ownership changes, or when growth is introduced, a different reality emerges:

- second-layer management is thin or non-existent
- processes are informal
- knowledge is not institutionalized

Even well-structured deals try to bridge this.

Earnouts, rollovers, transition periods.

But these mechanisms often shift risk rather than eliminate it.

The downside is rarely in the numbers.

It sits in:

- who holds relationships
- who makes decisions
- and what remains when they step back

What appears stable is often just untested.
