Most diligence starts with revenue and ends with legal. That is backwards for repeatable outcomes.

If you cannot state—in one sentence—what behavior the economic structure rewards, you do not yet understand the deal. The incentive map is not a appendix slide; it is the forecast. When carry, earn‑outs, or ratchets misalign operators and capital, diligence keeps praising “quality of earnings” while the real story is already written.

I look for a narrow set of tells: who can slow a decision without consequence, where information is bottled up by design, and which downside is socially “unthinkable” so it never gets modeled. Those are the nodes where outcomes actually diverge.

This is not cynicism. Markets clear faster when incentives are legible. My bias is toward structures that make the right move the easy move—because in private transactions, easy moves are the only ones that reliably happen.
