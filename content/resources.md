## Frameworks

**Incentive map (one page).**  
*Why it matters:* If you cannot draw the reward loop, you cannot predict what people will do when stressed. I use this before “strategic fit” conversations—because fit is often a story incentives disprove.

**Kill-criteria memo.**  
*Why it matters:* Stopping is a skill. A short list of observable falsifiers keeps politics from masquerading as patience. Most failures are-funded optimism wearing a diligence badge.

**Counterparty bottlenecks.**  
*Why it matters:* Deals are not decided in conference rooms; they are decided where approval chains narrow. Naming the bottleneck early prevents fake milestones later.

## Mental models

**Chesterton’s fence (borrowed).**  
*Why it matters:* Before changing a legacy commercial practice, understand why it survived embarrassment. Some “stupid” processes are insurance policies with a claims history you do not yet see.

**Via negativa.**  
*Why it matters:* In private markets, avoiding certain failure modes beats optimizing upside stories—especially when liquidity is conditional.

## References

*Daniel Kahneman — work on judgment under uncertainty.* Useful when teams confuse consensus for clarity.  
*John Kay — *Obliquity*.* A clean antidote to “maximize the spreadsheet” energy.  
*Richard Zeckhauser — investing under incomplete information.* Helpful language for staging commitment when data will always be imperfect.
