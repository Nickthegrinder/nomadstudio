Roll-up where the contracts were the risk

context

industry: B2B services (facilities-adjacent)

situation: Founder retiring; four bolt‑ons already assembled; customer concentration masked by “diversified” revenue lines.

capital involved

~$45M equity + ~$30M asset-backed facility (illustrative; rounded)

what happened

The outside narrative was operational improvement. The inside work was reconstructing renewals and change-of-control triggers scattered across predecessors—then stress-testing what was actually enforceable versus what was politely assumed.

outcome

Stabilized revenue retention above plan; exit 24–30 months later in line with sponsor hurdle rates (specific multiple withheld; composite anonymized).

insight

Most participants overweighted “synergy” spreadsheets and underweighted document dispersion—the hidden leverage of counterparties who could delay consent without breaching anything obvious.

The boring part of the job was inventory: thousands of pages, dozens of templates, a few killer clauses hiding in “standard” schedules. The interesting part was watching how incentives changed once everyone could see the same map.
